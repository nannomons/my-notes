> "Women have minds, and souls, as well as hearts; ambition and talent as well as beauty and I'm sick of being told that love is all a woman is fit for. But... I am so lonely." —Jo March

# Summary
Sisters Jo, Meg, Amy, and Beth navigate post-war life. And fuck the patriarchy! Jo wanted to be a writer, Meg an actress, Beth a musician. I actually don't know what Amy wanted to be but whatever I never really liked her anyway. NVM just remembered she liked painting I think? I could not care less I NEVER liked her character and say whatever you will about what that says about me.

It's a little confusing, because it flips from past and present and future as well as a fictional version of their lives because Jo ends up writing their lives into a book titled Little Women except its ending panders to the status quo—e.g. "every woman must marry or die by the end," SO FORGIVE ME if I misinterpreted a certain act as reality. 

With that said, I believe the story is, the four of them have dreams, but only Jo—with her pride and determination—has any drive to actually pursue them. 

Meg, instead of pursuing acting, decided to party around (in the very conservative "ball" stuff sense of the word) in order to marry a rich man Angelica Schuyler-style, but meets John something who's poor as FUCK but they fall in love anyway. 

Beth dies due to monkey pox? If I remember correctly? And truly genuinely everyone's distraught because she's the only likeable person in that whole house. 

Now, Jo always wanted to pursue writing and HATESSS the concept of being "married off" to a man, EXCEPT this guy named Teddy—or Laurie as what literally everyone else but JO calls him, oh GOD they even got the nickname only they call each other—who she was LITERALLY fated to fall in love with—DID YOU SEE THAT OPENING SCENE WITH THEM DANCING IN THE SHADOWS AND STUFF CZ NEITHER OF THEM KNEW HOW TO DANCE AND THEY HAD TO BE HIDDEN AND OH MY GODDDD?? They were LITERALLY made to fall in love but Jo was like "I can't be married; I have dreams and a future and I'm made to write stories people will resonate with!" 

So Laurie ends up marrying Amy, who was in love with him even while he was in love with Jo. YEAH. I can't believe this shit either. 

NO CAUSE HERE'S WHAT HAPPENED. You know that quote I wrote earlier, with the "Oh, God, I'm so lonely." or whatever?? She ends up talking to Marmee (that's what they call their mom) and she goes on this rant of, like, I have dreams and I have ambitions and I'm more than a wife. Women are more than wives, they're all these things and oh so capable, but oh God, I'm so lonely. I don't love Laurie, and I know we're not meant to be together, because we're both rowdy and loud and we disagree, but if he asks again, if he wants me to marry him, I'll say yes. 

FUCK MY STUPID BAKA LIFE YOU HEAR MEEEEEEEEEEEEE. FUCK ME.

AND SHE WROTE THIS LETTER, SEE? SHE WROTE THIS LETTER GOING "I realize I love you," but oh no, Laurie ends up going back to her home, because SURPRISE him and Amy are engaged. FUCK. And he says this, guess what? He says "You were right, I'm here to say you were right. I love you in a way *I* could *only* love *you*. But I love Amy too." WHAT THE FUCK IS YOUR PROBLEM. You arrive to Amy's party drunk, and she says she's been waiting for hours, and she says she's gonna marry this other rich guy, but she still loves HIM, and he says no, don't marry that other guy, because I love you, knowing damn well he doesn't, because Jo is ALL he ever talks about, and we know it, and Amy knows it, but Amy dumps other rich guy, because she loves Laurie, knowing DAMN WELL Laurie loves HER SISTER, where the FUCK is the self-respect in this damn household?? 

Amy has done NOTHING right in this goddamn movie and Jo is stronger than me because I would have NEVER forgiven her for anything she has done ever at any point in time. AND FUCK LAURIE.

Oh yeah, so this is all reflected in "Little Women," as in, the story Jo March wrote, EXCEPT, because the publisher man won't accept it if it didn't have a "good ending" (the original ending was that in-universe Jo, the main character, never ends up with Laurie nor the blunt immigrant professor guy both of whom Jo was DESTINED to end up with Wattpad-style) she re-wrote the ending to be that Jo would run after professor guy at the terminal and they kiss mwamwa all-sweet like. But in reality she never did that I think. ACTUALLY I'LL LOOK IT UP WAIT.

JOHN WAS THE GUY TEACHING LAURIE I REMEMBER NOW. Also, Beth got scarlet fever, not monkey pox.
