Cations are **positively charged ions**. They are formed when **[[Metals]] lose its [[Electrons]]**. They lose one or more than one electron. It has fewer electrons than protons. Therefore, they possess a net positive charge. Some examples of cations are Calcium (Ca2+), Potassium (K+), hydrogen (H+).
