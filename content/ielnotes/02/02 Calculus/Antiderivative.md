> In differential calculus, we learned that the derivative $f'$ of a function $f$ gives the instantaneous rate of change of $f$ for a given input. Now we're going the other way! For any rate function $f$, its **antiderivative** $F$ gives the *accumulated value of the quantity whose rate is described by* $f$.
