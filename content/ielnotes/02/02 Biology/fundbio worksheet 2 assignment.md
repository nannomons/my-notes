**ACROSS**  
1. The observable traits of an organism (e.g., tall, short). — [[Phenotype]]  
2. Scientist who developed the Hardy–Weinberg principle. — [[Hardy-Weinberg Principle]]  
3. When neither allele is fully dominant, resulting in a blended trait. — [[Incomplete Dominance]]  
4. The proportion of a specific allele or genotype in a population. — [[Allele Frequency]] and [[Genotype Frequency]] 
5. **The principle that alleles separate during gamete formation.** — [[Mendel's Law of Segregation]] 
6. A change in DNA that creates new alleles. — [[Mutation]]  
7. A grid used to predict the outcomes of genetic crosses. — [[Punnett Square]]  
8. When both alleles are expressed equally (e.g., AB blood type). — [[Codominance]]  
9. **A state where allele frequencies in a population remain constant.** — [[Equilibrium]]
10. **Differences among individuals in a population.** — [[Variation]]
11. Change in allele frequencies in a population over time. — [[Evolution]]  
12. Scientist who developed the Hardy–Weinberg principle. — [[Hardy-Weinberg Principle]]  
  
**DOWN**  
1. The genetic makeup of an organism (e.g., AA, Aa, aa). — [[Genotype]]  
2. Movement of individuals and their alleles into or out of a population. — [[Migration]]  
3. The process where traits that improve survival or reproduction become more common. — [[Natural Selection]]   
4. A group of interbreeding individuals of the same species. — [[Population]]  
5. A cross that studies the inheritance of one trait. — [[Monohybrid Cross]]  
6. Having two identical alleles for a trait (AA or aa). — [[Homozygous]]  
7. Different versions of a gene (e.g., A or a). — [[Allele]]  
8. **The principle that alleles of different genes sort randomly during gamete formation.** — [[Law of Independent Assortment]]
9. Having two different alleles for a trait (Aa). — [[Heterozygous]]  
10. Random changes in allele frequencies, especially in small populations. — [[Genetic Drift]]
11. An allele that is only expressed if two copies are present. — [[Recessive]]  
12. The total collection of alleles in a population [pool]. — [[Gene Pool]]  
13. A trait controlled by two or more genes (e.g., height, skin color). — [[Polygenic Inheritance]]  
14. **When one gene influences multiple traits.** —  [[Pleiotropy]]
15. **When one gene affects or masks the expression of another gene.** — [[Epistasis]]
16. A unit of heredity that carries information for a trait. — [[Gene]]  
17. A cross that studies the inheritance of two traits. — [[Dihybrid Cross]]  
18. An allele that is expressed even if only one copy is present. — [[Dominant]]
