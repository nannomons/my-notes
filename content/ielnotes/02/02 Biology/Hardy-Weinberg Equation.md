The **Hardy-Weinberg equation** is used to calculate the genetic variation of a population at equilibrium. 

$$p^2+2pq+q^2=1$$ 
Where:
- $p$ is the frequency of the *dominant allele* AA,
- $q$ is the frequency of the *recessive allele* aa,
- $p^2$ represents the frequency of *homozygous dominant individuals* AA,
- $2pq$ represents the frequency of *heterozygous individuals* Aa, and
- $q^2$ represents the frequency of *homozygous recessive individuals* aa.
