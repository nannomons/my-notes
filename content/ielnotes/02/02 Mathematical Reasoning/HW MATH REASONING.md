**Claim:** $\mathbb{Z}_{E}$ is closed under multiplication. If $n,m \in \mathbb{Z}_{E}$, then $nm \in \mathbb{Z}_{E}$. 

**Proof:** 
Let $n,m \in \mathbb{Z}_{E}$. By definition, $n=2k$ for some $k \in \mathbb{Z}$ and $m=2l$ for some $l \in \mathbb{Z}$.

Then, $nm=(2k)(2l)$ ; Substitution
$nm = 2(2kl)$ Assoc

Since $2kl \in \mathbb{Z}$ by closure for multiplication, so $nm=2p$ where $p=2kl$. 
Thus, $nm \in \mathbb{Z}_{E}$ for some $2kl \in \mathbb{Z}$.

**CLAIM:** If $a|b$ and $a|c$, then $a|(b+c)$.

**PROOF:**
Let $a,b,c \in \mathbb{Z}$ where $a|b$ and $a|c$. 
	By definition, $b=ak$ for some $k \in \mathbb{Z}$.
	By definition, $c=al$ for some $l \in \mathbb{R}$

	$b+c=ak+al$
	Substitution
	$b+c=a(k+l)$ Associativity
	Note that $k+l \in \mathbb{Z}$ by closure property, thus $a|(b+c)$ for some $m=k+l \in \mathbb{Z}$.
