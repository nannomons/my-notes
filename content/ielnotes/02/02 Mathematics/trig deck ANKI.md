Q: Define $\sec$. 
A: The **secant** is the *reciprocal of the cosine* which is calculated through the ratio $$\frac{hypotenuse}{adjacent}$$

Q: Define $\csc$.
A: The **cosecant** is the *reciprocal of the sine* and is calculated with the ratio $$
\frac{hypotenuse}{opposite}
$$

Q: Define $\cot$
A: The **cotangent** is the *reciprocal of the tangent* and is calculated with the ratio $$
\frac{adjacent}{opposite}
$$

Q: How to calculate **radians** given the *degrees*? 
A: $$
\text{Angle in Radians} = \dfrac{\pi}{180^\circ}\cdot\text{Angle in Degrees}
$$
<!--ID: 1756450589151-->


Q: How to calculate **degrees** given the *radians*?
A: $$
\text{Angle in Degrees} = \dfrac{180^\circ}{\pi}\cdot\text{Angle in Radians}
$$

Q: What is $30^\circ$ in *radians*?
A: $$\frac{\pi}{6}$$

Multiply $(\frac{\pi}{180^\circ})(\frac{1}{30^\circ})$; essentially, $\pi$ over $\frac{180}{30}$, so that would be $\frac{\pi}{6}$.
