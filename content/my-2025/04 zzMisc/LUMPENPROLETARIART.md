> In Marxist theory, the Lumpenproletariat is the underclass devoid of class consciousness. Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels coined the word in the 1840s and used it to refer to the unthinking lower strata of society exploited by reactionary and counter-revolutionary forces, particularly in the context of the revolutions of 1848. They dismissed the revolutionary potential of the Lumpenproletariat and contrasted it with the proletariat. Among other groups, criminals, vagabonds, and prostitutes are usually included in this category. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lumpenproletariat)

### Marxist Perspective

- Marx and Engels viewed the lumpenproletariat as a parasitic group, unable to contribute positively to revolutionary movements. They believed this group acted out of self-interest and could be easily manipulated.
