# History of Philosophy
- **Ancient Greece.** Buddhism and Jainism in Asia; Philosophy in Greece. 
- **Phylos vs. Mythos**: Science *(Scientific and analytical)* vs. Storytelling *(Literary like Homer)*. 
- ***"Philosophia"*** *(Greek)* meaning "the love of wisdom."
- ***"Philosophy"*** or the academic study of anything. Over time evolved into *the way of thinking about questions.* 

# Five Branches of Philosophy
1. [[02 Philosophy/Metaphysics]]: Studies the nature of reality.
2. [[02 Philosophy/Epistemology]]: Studies the nature of knowledge.
3. [[Ethics]]: Studies what evaluates human conduct. **Part of [[02 Philosophy/Value Theory]].**
4. [[Aesthetics]]: Studies the nature of beauty and art. **Part of [[02 Philosophy/Value Theory]].** 
5. [[02 Philosophy/Logic]]: Reasoning and shit; stuff like [[02 Philosophy/Fallacies]]. 

# On Rationality 
- [[Aristotle]] believed that [[02 Philosophy/Rationality]] is our distinguishing characteristic—what sets us apart from the beasts. Our **ability to reason and be persuaded** by [[02 Philosophy/Arguments]]. 
- [[Plato]] believed we had a [[02 Philosophy/Tripartite Soul]] and that humans were ruled by the rational part of the soul.

# Validity vs. Truth
[[02 Philosophy/Validity]] only states that **if the premises are true then the conclusion can't be false,** but that doesn't mean that the premises prove your conclusion to be correct. 

E.g.: All humans have tails. Socrates is a human. Therefore Socrates has a tail.

The argument is valid but Premise A is not true, therefore the conclusion may not be true.
