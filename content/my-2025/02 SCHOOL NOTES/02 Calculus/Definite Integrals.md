$$
\int_{a}^{b} f(x) \, dx
$$

This represents the area under $f(x)$ between $x = a$ (left x-axis) and $x=b$ (right x-axis). 

> There are the two ways we commonly think about definite integrals: they describe an accumulation of a quantity, so the entire definite integral gives us the **net change** in that quantity, not the **actual quantity**.

## On Inappropriate Units
 > Remember that if $r$ is a rate function measured in $\frac{QuantityA}{QuantityB}$, then its definite integral is measured in $QuantityA$.
